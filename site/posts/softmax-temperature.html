Why softmax temperature is just confidence, rebranded.

ml fundamentals masters

Spent the morning unlearning what I thought I knew about logits. Notes from chapter 4 of the deep learning book + a sketch I drew on the back of a napkin while waiting for k8s pods to schedule.

The short version

Temperature (τ) is a knob for how much you trust your model's raw outputs. High temperature = "I'm not sure, spread the probability around." Low temperature = "I'm very confident, sharpen the distribution."

What softmax actually does

Given a vector of logits z, softmax converts them to probabilities: 

softmax(z_i) = exp(z_i) / Σ exp(z_j)

When you introduce temperature, you divide each logit by τ before applying softmax: 

softmax(z_i / τ) = exp(z_i / τ) / Σ exp(z_j / τ)

Why this matters

When τ → 0, the distribution collapses to a one-hot vector (argmax). When τ → ∞, it approaches uniform. The "temperature" metaphor comes from statistical mechanics — high temperature = high entropy = more randomness.

The key insight: temperature doesn't change what the model learned. It changes how decisively the model acts on what it learned.

The napkin sketch

I drew three softmax distributions at τ=0.5, τ=1.0, and τ=2.0 over a 4-class problem. The visual difference is striking — the low-temperature version looks like a spike, the high-temperature one looks almost flat.

Takeaway

Next time someone says "adjust the temperature," just read it as "adjust the confidence." It clicks faster that way.
